Why this matters

Coastal and Marine Modelling

Coastal and marine modelling is needed to understand complex water movement in coastal zones, estuaries, ports, beaches, and marine infrastructure areas. The analysis supports safer coastal planning by evaluating tides, waves, storm surge, tsunami, sediment movement, and coastal inundation.

Main purpose: to support coastal engineering design, marine hazard assessment, sediment management, and planning for resilient coastal and offshore infrastructure.

Key Questions Addressed

  • How do tides, waves, and currents behave around the project area?
  • Which coastal zones are exposed to inundation or erosion risk?
  • How will sediment move after construction or dredging activities?
  • How can coastal structures affect hydrodynamic and sediment transport patterns?

Typical Analysis

Hydrodynamic Modelling

Simulation of tides, currents, water levels, wave-current interaction, storm surge, and coastal circulation.

Sediment and Hazard Analysis

Assessment of erosion, deposition, sediment transport, tsunami impact, and coastal inundation risk.

Why It Is Important

Coastal areas are highly dynamic and sensitive to natural hazards and human intervention. Engineering decisions without hydrodynamic analysis may create unexpected erosion, sedimentation, flooding, or navigation problems.

Numerical modelling provides a scientific basis for evaluating coastal protection, port development, reclamation, dredging, and marine infrastructure before implementation.

Expected Outputs

  • Tide, wave, current, and water level simulation results.
  • Coastal inundation and hazard maps.
  • Sediment transport, erosion, and deposition patterns.
  • Scenario comparison for coastal structures or project alternatives.
  • Technical recommendations for coastal planning and marine engineering.

Application Areas

This service can be applied to coastal protection, port and harbour planning, marine infrastructure design, dredging impact assessment, tsunami inundation studies, sediment management, and coastal zone planning.
