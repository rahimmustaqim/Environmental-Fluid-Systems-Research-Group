Why this matters

Flood Modelling and River Capacity Analysis

Flood modelling is essential for understanding how rainfall, river flow, drainage capacity, land use, and infrastructure conditions interact during extreme events. Without proper modelling, flood risk may be underestimated, leading to insufficient river improvement plans, inadequate drainage systems, and higher exposure for communities and assets.

Main purpose: to support flood risk evaluation, river capacity assessment, inundation mapping, and engineering decision-making for safer and more resilient water infrastructure.

Key Questions Addressed

  • Which river sections are most likely to overflow during design rainfall events?
  • How far and how deep will floodwater spread across the surrounding area?
  • Which improvement option gives the most effective reduction in flood risk?
  • How does land-use change or climate-related rainfall increase affect future flood hazard?

Typical Analysis

Hydrological Analysis

Rainfall-runoff transformation, catchment response analysis, design flood estimation, and hydrograph development.

Hydraulic Modelling

River flow simulation, water level assessment, inundation mapping, overflow analysis, and river improvement testing.

Why It Is Important

Flood modelling provides quantitative evidence for infrastructure planning. It helps engineers and decision-makers identify whether existing rivers, channels, culverts, or drainage systems have sufficient capacity under current and future conditions.

The analysis is also important for comparing mitigation alternatives, such as river widening, channel normalization, retention ponds, diversion channels, embankments, pump systems, or improved drainage networks.

Expected Outputs

  • Flood depth and flood extent maps.
  • Water level profiles along river channels.
  • River capacity and overflow assessment.
  • Comparison of existing and proposed improvement scenarios.
  • Technical recommendations for flood mitigation and planning.

Application Areas

This service can be applied to urban flood assessment, river basin planning, drainage masterplan development, infrastructure impact assessment, flood mitigation design, and climate impact studies.
