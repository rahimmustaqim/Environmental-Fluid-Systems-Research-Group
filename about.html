About the group

A research-led engineering group focused on environmental fluid systems.

Environmental Fluid Systems Research Group combines numerical modelling, field and geospatial data analysis, and engineering interpretation to support science-based decision making.

Our Focus

We work across hydrology, hydraulics, hydrogeology, climate analysis, coastal engineering, and computational fluid dynamics. The goal is to provide reliable technical analysis for water-related engineering and environmental challenges.

The group is suitable for academic collaboration, engineering consulting support, technical analysis, modelling assistance, and research-oriented project development.

  • Numerical modelling for complex water and fluid systems
  • GIS and data-driven analysis for engineering decisions
  • Clear reporting, visualization, and technical communication
  • Research-based approach with practical engineering outputs

Core Values

Scientific accuracy. Model assumptions, input data, and limitations should be communicated transparently.

Engineering relevance. Outputs should support real decision making, not only visual results.

Collaboration. We aim to work with researchers, consultants, agencies, and project stakeholders.

Resilience. Our work supports water, coastal, and environmental infrastructure planning under changing conditions.
