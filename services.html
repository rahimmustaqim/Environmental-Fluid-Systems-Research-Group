Business Scope

Engineering and modelling services for water, coastal, and environmental systems.

We provide numerical modelling, data analysis, technical interpretation, and decision-support services for hydrology, hydraulics, coastal systems, groundwater, climate impact, and environmental fluid dynamics.

Our Business Scope

Each service scope is designed to support engineering planning, risk assessment, infrastructure evaluation, and science-based decision making.

Hydrology · Climate

Hydrology and Climate Analysis

Analysis of rainfall, runoff, watershed response, flood frequency, and climate-related changes to support water resources planning and resilient infrastructure development.

The Purpose

To understand how rainfall and climate variability affect runoff, flood potential, water availability, and long-term infrastructure performance.

We Help Analyse

  • Rainfall characteristics, design storm, and extreme rainfall conditions.
  • Rainfall-runoff response and watershed contribution to river discharge.
  • Flood frequency, peak discharge, and return-period-based hydrological scenarios.
  • Climate change impact on rainfall, runoff, and future water-related hazards.

Typical Outputs

Rainfall-runoff hydrograph
Design flood discharge
Flood frequency summary
Climate scenario analysis
1
Collect Data

Rainfall, watershed, land use, river, and climate datasets.

2
Build Model

Develop hydrological models and define design scenarios.

3
Analyse Results

Evaluate runoff, discharge, water availability, and risk indicators.

4
Support Decision

Provide technical interpretation for planning and engineering design.

Flood Risk
Reservoir Planning

Infrastructure

Hydraulic · Flood

Flood and River Engineering

Hydraulic modelling for river flow, flood inundation, drainage performance, river capacity, overflow risk, and improvement scenarios.

The Purpose

To evaluate how rivers and drainage systems behave under normal and extreme flow conditions, so flood risks and mitigation options can be clearly understood.

We Help Analyse

  • River water levels, flow capacity, overflow locations, and floodplain behaviour.
  • Flood inundation depth, extent, velocity, and affected infrastructure.
  • Hydraulic structures, bridges, culverts, channels, and drainage systems.
  • Alternative mitigation designs such as diversion channels and river improvements.

Typical Outputs

Flood inundation map
Water level profile
Velocity distribution
Scenario comparison
1
Define Problem

Identify river, drainage, flood, or hydraulic capacity issues.

2
Set Scenarios

Prepare design flood, boundary conditions, and geometry data.

3
Run Model

Simulate flow, water level, velocity, and inundation extent.

4
Recommend Options

Compare alternatives and support engineering decisions.

Urban Flooding
River Capacity

Coastal · Marine

Coastal and Marine Engineering

Coastal hydrodynamic and sediment transport modelling for tides, waves, storm surge, tsunami, coastal inundation, shoreline change, and marine hazard assessment.

The Purpose

To understand how coastal systems respond to natural forces and human interventions, so risks can be reduced and coastal decisions can be made with confidence.

We Help Analyse

  • Waves, tides, currents, water levels, and coastal circulation patterns.
  • Storm surge, tsunami propagation, and coastal inundation hazards.
  • Sediment transport, erosion, deposition, and shoreline response.
  • Port, harbour, navigation channel, reclamation, and coastal protection performance.

Current vector field
Water level distribution
Sediment transport map

CFD · Environmental Fluid Dynamics

CFD and Environmental Fluid Dynamics

Computational fluid dynamics simulation for complex flow behaviour, turbulence, dispersion, mixing, and interaction between fluid motion and engineering structures.

The Purpose

To virtually test flow behaviour before physical implementation, helping engineers understand turbulence, recirculation, dispersion, and local hydraulic performance.

We Help Analyse

  • Flow separation, vortices, recirculation zones, and turbulence structures.
  • Fluid-structure interaction around hydraulic, coastal, and environmental structures.
  • Dispersion, mixing, scalar transport, sediment movement, or pollutant spreading.
  • Model setup, mesh generation, boundary conditions, and simulation interpretation.

Typical Outputs

Velocity contour
Streamline pattern
Turbulence field
Dispersion plume
1
Define Case

Set geometry, flow problem, objective, and physical assumptions.

2
Prepare Model

Create mesh, boundary conditions, materials, and numerical settings.

3
Simulate Flow

Run flow, turbulence, mixing, and structure interaction analysis.

4
Explain Results

Translate simulation outputs into engineering interpretation.

Applications

Bridge Pier Flow
Hydraulic Structure
Coastal Flow
Pollutant Mixing
Design Testing
